Monthly Eco Planner & Tips

January

Make your home eco-friendly

Wrap your water heater in an insulating jacket.

Replace your bulbs with energy efficient LEDs.

Service your boiler regularly.

February

Eat lower on the food chain

Fruits, vegetables, beans and nuts have much lower carbon footprints.

Try meatless Mondays to save on greenhouse-gas equal to 80 miles cycling instead of driving.

March

Donate gently used goods

Recycling clothes and shoes helps the environment, just the same as recycling bottles, papers and plastic can.

Donating clothes also help reduce landfill sites, water usage and your carbon footprint.

April

Reduce, Reuse, Recycle

Reduce your food waste by buying only what you need.

Maintain and repair clothes and appliances so they won't have to be replaced frequently.

Borrow, rent or share items that are used infrequently.

May

Buy local

Find out what's in season and plan your meals based on what's available locally.

Use ebay, Facebook marketplace and Gumtree. Subscribe to Oddbox.

Join your local Community Supported Agriculture (CSA) network.

June

Use less plastic

Carry a reusable water bottle.

Avoid over-packaged products.

Say no to plastic straws and disposable cutleries.

Carry a shopping bag.

Avoid microbeads.

July

Shop sustainably

Go for quality over quantity.

Choose products that have been responsibly sourced and produced without the use of harmful chemicals.

Consider or upgrade domestic appliances or electronics with the best energy efficiency rating that you can afford.

August

Choose eco-friendly vacation

Look at eco-tourism options.

Choose the most environmentally friendly form of transport.

Choose the most environmentally accommodation.

Be a responsible guest - respect the local environment and limit energy use.

September

Gardening for the environment

Plant a tree.

Water your plants the way nature intended - use water that falls onto your roof.

Go peat-free. When we take peat for our gardens, carbon is released and habitats are damaged.
